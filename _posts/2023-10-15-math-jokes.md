Mathematical humor is a genre unto itself. The jokes reveal something true about how mathematicians think, what they value (precision, abstraction, rigor), and what they find absurd.

Here is a curated collection, roughly ordered by technical prerequisites.

---

## Level 0: No Prerequisites

**Q:** Why did the mathematician name their dog Cauchy?

**A:** Because it left a residue at every pole.

---

**Q:** What's the contour integral of Western Europe?

**A:** Zero — all the poles are in Eastern Europe.

---

## Level 1: Calculus Required

**An infinite number of mathematicians walk into a bar.** The first orders 1 beer. The second orders 1/2 a beer. The third orders 1/4. The bartender pours 2 beers and says, "You lot should know your limits."

---

**Q:** What did the zero say to the eight?

**A:** Nice belt.

*(Geometric humor.)*

---

## Level 2: Some Real Analysis

**A topologist and a geometer argue about a coffee mug.** The topologist says it's a donut. The geometer disagrees. They never speak again.

---

**A student asks:** "Will this be on the exam?" 

**The professor:** "The exam is the closure of this course. Everything in the closure is on the exam."

---

## Level 3: Graduate Level

**Q:** What is a proof?

**A:** A proof is a proof. What kind of a proof? A proof is a proof, and when you have a good proof, it's because it's proven. — *Jean Chrétien, inadvertent logician.*

---

**An engineer, a physicist, and a mathematician** are asked to fence the most land with the least fencing.

The engineer builds a square.

The physicist builds a circle.

The mathematician builds a tiny fence around themselves and declares, "I define this to be the outside."

---

**A mathematician and their spouse** are driving and get lost. The spouse says, "Stop and ask for directions." The mathematician says, "I know exactly where we are — we're somewhere on the real line."

---

## The Meta-Joke

The best mathematical jokes aren't really jokes — they're **surprising truths** dressed as punchlines. The contour integral joke only works because the Residue Theorem is real. The limit joke only works because the geometric series $\sum 1/2^n = 2$ is actually true.

This is, in miniature, what mathematics is: structures so well-designed that their consequences surprise even the people who built them.

$$e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$$

The universe's best punchline. Still not sure who set it up.
